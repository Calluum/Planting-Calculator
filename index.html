A note on the twin distance. Whether widening the twin gap adds stems depends on what your ripper holds fixed. If the pass-to-pass interval is fixed, the band pitch above should match it, and moving the twins apart only repositions the two rows inside each pass — density is unchanged. The twin gap adds stems only when the between-pair gap stays fixed and the twins widen on top of it.
